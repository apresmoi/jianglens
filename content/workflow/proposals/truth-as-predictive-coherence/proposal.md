## Proposed Boundary

Record **truth as predictive coherence** as a reviewed atlas relation, not a standalone public concept page. The mechanism is that Jiang uses prediction as a truth test in several registers that should not be flattened together.

In the poetic and metaphysical register, truth contains time. A prophet is not mainly a fortune-teller. A prophet speaks truth because truth is the universe, and the universe holds past, present, and future together (`video:predictive-history-ft2cuowguyc@transcript:v1#seg-0027`). Literature can therefore foretell because it speaks an eternal moral structure rather than because the poet guesses tomorrow (`video:predictive-history-ft2cuowguyc@transcript:v1#seg-0028`, `video:predictive-history-ft2cuowguyc@transcript:v1#seg-0030`, `video:predictive-history-ft2cuowguyc@transcript:v1#seg-0031`).

In the historical and modeling register, prediction is a test of narrative coherence, not proof. A theory approaches truth when it connects the past, explains the present, and predicts the future (`video:predictive-history-wfwizn3qopg@transcript:v1#seg-0001`, `video:predictive-history-wfwizn3qopg@transcript:v1#seg-0002`). But Jiang explicitly cautions that prediction does not establish causation or make the theory certain (`video:predictive-history-wfwizn3qopg@transcript:v1#seg-0002`, `video:predictive-history-wfwizn3qopg@transcript:v1#seg-0003`).

This seed should remain narrower than [Game Theory](/lens/game-theory/), [Living School For Psychohistory](/lens/living-school-for-psychohistory/), [How Poetry Creates Civilization](/lens/how-poetry-creates-civilization/), and [Eschatology As Script](/lens/eschatology/). Game theory owns player/incentive analysis and the caveat that visible models invite intervention. Living-school owns the institutional formation of people who can build and govern psychohistory. Poetry owns language, beauty, memory, and civilizational world-making. Eschatology owns prophecy as a script that coordinates geopolitical action. This seed would name a cross-cutting epistemic rule: prediction matters because a true account should bind past, present, and future, but the kind of truth being tested changes by register.

## Source Pressure

The earliest strong source is **2024-06-13**, the final Psychohistory class. Jiang introduces AI-assisted future prediction as a model-building practice: define the output, clean the data, and use a working algorithm (`video:predictive-history-s-k6eswheqa@transcript:v1#seg-0018`). He then gives an example forecast about Iran war pressure, turns it into a model, and says the model must be refined by going back through historical cases such as Sicily and Barbarossa (`video:predictive-history-s-k6eswheqa@transcript:v1#seg-0019`, `video:predictive-history-s-k6eswheqa@transcript:v1#seg-0020`). The important point is recursive: prediction requires historical testing, and historical testing refines the prediction model.

The **2024-09-05** Iliad source gives the metaphysical primitive. Jiang says that if an interpretation is true, it should let the reader understand the present scene, look back to how it came into being, and make predictions about the future (`video:predictive-history-ft2cuowguyc@transcript:v1#seg-0013`). The lecture then climbs into the stronger claim. Prophets bring the truth of the universe into language; poets, prophets, and teachers are one function (`video:predictive-history-ft2cuowguyc@transcript:v1#seg-0023`). A student asks whether prophets are really predicting the future, and Jiang answers by redefining prophecy: truth is the universe, beyond space and time, so truth includes what happened, what is happening, and what will happen (`video:predictive-history-ft2cuowguyc@transcript:v1#seg-0026`, `video:predictive-history-ft2cuowguyc@transcript:v1#seg-0027`). The moral example is Achilles: evil done outward returns inward, making the prophetic statement both prediction and judgment (`video:predictive-history-ft2cuowguyc@transcript:v1#seg-0028`).

The **2025-04-08** Poets and Prophets lecture intensifies the same metaphysics through Shelley. Poets are prophets because they have a divine connection to the universe (`video:predictive-history-xrp407wsa0w@transcript:v1#seg-0015`). Poetry gives higher sight beyond ordinary time and space (`video:predictive-history-xrp407wsa0w@transcript:v1#seg-0032`). Poets may not know they are prophets; they speak because they must, because truth has to pass through them (`video:predictive-history-xrp407wsa0w@transcript:v1#seg-0034`). The lecture ends by saying poets are prophets because the future is speaking to the present, and their words create reality (`video:predictive-history-xrp407wsa0w@transcript:v1#seg-0036`, `video:predictive-history-xrp407wsa0w@transcript:v1#seg-0037`).

The **2025-06-13** direct-address source gives the project definition. Predictive History is a new history whose three goals are to connect past events into a coherent story, explain the present, and predict the future. If it does all three, Jiang calls it true history (`video:predictive-history-voqeteh6hko@transcript:v1#seg-0005`). The next segment ties this to Asimov's psychohistory: model past and present so as to predict the future, then build intellectual foundations for that possibility (`video:predictive-history-voqeteh6hko@transcript:v1#seg-0006`).

The **2025-12-18** Pax Judaica lecture gives the strongest caveat and therefore the best reason to keep this seed under review. Jiang proposes a theory of truth: a theory, idea, or narrative approaches truth when it connects the past, explains the present, and predicts the future (`video:predictive-history-wfwizn3qopg@transcript:v1#seg-0001`, `video:predictive-history-wfwizn3qopg@transcript:v1#seg-0002`). But he immediately limits the claim. Even if predicted events happen, correlation is not causation; the theory is not certainly correct, and he frames the surrounding material as speculation to be doubted (`video:predictive-history-wfwizn3qopg@transcript:v1#seg-0002`, `video:predictive-history-wfwizn3qopg@transcript:v1#seg-0003`).

## Why This Advances The Lens Map

The public lens already has the pieces but not the boundary. Poetry explains how truth, beauty, language, and memory make civilization. Living-school explains why Predictive History needs a school, model correction, human-heart variables, and democratic governance. Game theory explains how to find the real game and why a visible model can change the board. Eschatology explains prophecy as a script that actors can perform.

What is missing is a boundary that prevents these pieces from blurring. Jiang's word "prediction" can mean model forecast, moral consequence, timeless truth, story test, or geopolitical script. The reviewed atlas relation gives future agents a controlled routing boundary: Jiang has a reusable epistemic pressure around past-present-future coherence, but the active register should still decide which public page owns a source moment.

## Boundary Notes

Do not merge this seed into Game Theory unless the source is about players, rules, incentives, coordination, or model visibility. The truth-as-prediction question is wider than strategic analysis.

Do not merge this seed into Poetry unless the source is about the poet, prophet, teacher, beauty, timeless truth, or language carrying the universe. Predictive History's modeling register is not the same as poetic prophecy.

Do not merge this seed into Living School unless the source is about institutional formation, model refinement, democratic psychohistory, or the human preconditions for prediction. The school page should not become an epistemology catchall.

Do not merge this seed into Eschatology unless prophecy becomes an operative script that coordinates actors. Truth containing time is not identical to end-times mobilization.

## Possible Future Public Shape

If later promoted, the page should likely be titled **Truth As Predictive Coherence** rather than **Prophecy**. A "prophecy" title would over-attract eschatology and make the method sound like fortune-telling. The public page should probably include:

- a section on **poetic truth**: prophet, poet, and teacher speak timeless truth where past, present, and future converge;
- a section on **model truth**: historical narrative approaches truth when it connects past, explains present, and predicts future;
- a section on **correction and humility**: prediction has to be tested, failed, refined, and distinguished from proof;
- a section on **danger**: a theory can orient attention without proving causation, and visible predictions can become material inside the game;
- a diagnostics section asking what kind of prediction is operating in a source.

## Candidate Lens Points

The first candidate is now public as an atlas relation; the others remain held until a future page or relation needs them.

- `truth-prediction-coherence-atlas`  
  Public in `website/src/content/docs/lens.md`: Prediction becomes a truth test only after the lens names the register: poetic truth contains time, historical models earn confidence through past-present-future coherence, strategic prediction can alter the game, and successful forecasts still do not prove causation.
- `truth-contains-time`  
  In Jiang's poetic register, truth is eternal because past, present, and future converge in it; prophecy is speaking truth, not guessing tomorrow.
- `true-history-predictive-coherence`  
  In Jiang's historical register, an account approaches true history when it connects the past, explains the present, and predicts the future.
- `prediction-is-not-proof`: Prediction can strengthen a model without proving causation; a useful narrative must still remain caveated, doubtable, and corrigible.
- `psychohistory-refines-through-testing`: Psychohistory develops when predictions become models that are tested against historical cases and revised rather than protected as prophecy.

## Promotion Conditions

The cross-page review promoted the seed only as an atlas relation. Promote beyond that compact public surface only if future source review can answer:

- Does Jiang consistently use "truth" as a past-present-future coherence test across literature, history, and geopolitics?
- Does he distinguish poetic prophecy from model prediction strongly enough for one page to hold both?
- Can the page give readers diagnostics without making every successful forecast sound divinely true?
- Can the page preserve the Pax Judaica caveat that prediction is not proof?

Until then, keep this as a reviewed seed and use it to guide future boundary decisions.
