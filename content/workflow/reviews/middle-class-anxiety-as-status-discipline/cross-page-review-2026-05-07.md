## Decision

Hold **Middle-Class Anxiety As Status Discipline** as a reviewed proposal seed. Do not promote it to a public page or atlas relation yet.

The seed identifies a real cross-source mechanism: unstable middle-class status makes proof of worth continuous, so wealth, work, cleanliness, household order, taste, art, education, conformity, and consumer purpose become signs that must be renewed. That mechanism is strong enough to preserve as a routing rule. It is not yet strong enough to justify a new public surface because each active layer already has a stronger public home.

The best current use is boundary discipline. When a future source names middle-class anxiety as status proof, route the phrase by active mechanism unless the whole status ecology is present:

- Use **Attention Capture** when anxiety sends attention back to work, debt, focus, consumer comparison, or capital extraction.
- Use **Power As Alchemy** when money, individualism, or status objects become lived reality through trained imagination.
- Use **Education As A Soul Game** when school, parenting, credentials, or childhood pressure form the person.
- Use **Mass Society** when the issue is feeding, organizing, occupying, spiritually stabilizing, or controlling millions.
- Use **Religion As Administrative Filter** when doctrine, method, language, ritual, or exam selects officeholders.
- Use **World-Making Media** when art, literature, theater, or image-making builds a shared world rather than specifically containing respectable anxiety.
- Use **Game Theory** when status is being analyzed as payoff, rule-set, rank incentive, or competition board.
- Use **Human Heart** when Jiang's pressure is love, relation, purpose, guilt, forgiveness, or civilizational measure.

Use the middle-class anxiety seed only when the passage carries the full class-status proof mechanism: status is unstable, falling is imaginable, respectability needs visible proof, and cultural or institutional forms absorb that pressure.

## Promotion Conditions

Do not promote this seed until at least one of these conditions is met:

1. A later source adds another non-Dutch, non-Tocqueville recurrence where middle-class status proof, cultural containment, and political conformity appear together.
2. Existing public pages begin duplicating the same middle-class proof mechanism across attention, education, media, and mass society prose.

If promotion becomes necessary, the likely form is an atlas relation before a public page. A standalone page should wait until the source trail can support reader diagnostics without becoming a general class critique.
