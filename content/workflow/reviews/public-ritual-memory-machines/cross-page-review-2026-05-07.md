## Question

Should **Public Ritual Memory Machines** move from proposal seed into a public concept page, become an atlas relation, or remain a workflow boundary for future lens work?

## Decision

Hold **Public Ritual Memory Machines** as a reviewed proposal seed. Do not promote it to a public concept page or atlas relation yet.

The seed names a real Jiang mechanism: public forms can manufacture collective consciousness when staged participation, spectatorship, judgment, sacrifice, repetition, or performance makes a community remember and imagine together. The Viking funeral source makes that mechanism unusually explicit by comparing Viking funeral, Roman triumph, and Greek theater. But the current public lens already has strong neighboring homes for the active layers. A public page now would likely duplicate those pages before the corpus proves that public ritual needs its own reader-facing surface.

The seed should remain available as a routing rule: use it when the source pressure is the public form itself, not merely theater, sacrifice, sacred architecture, national ceremony, or story coordination by keyword.

## Reader / World-Model Review

### World-Making Media

The public atlas already owns the symbolic-media layer. It places Greek theater inside World-Making Media because public performance trains citizens to step back from inherited stories, switch perspectives, debate power and justice, and form a democratic mind (`website/src/content/docs/lens.md:99`, `website/src/content/docs/lens.md:103`). Poetry, scripture, story, and film are already mapped as forms that create worlds, coordinate action, and sometimes detach institutions from material constraint (`website/src/content/docs/lens.md:91`, `website/src/content/docs/lens.md:107`, `website/src/content/docs/lens.md:113`).

Public ritual should not steal theater from this family. It only becomes relevant when a source explicitly compares theater with other civic ceremonies or asks how a public staged form makes collective memory durable.

### Human Heart

Human Heart owns tragedy as democratic heart-knowledge: theater lets a city see revenge, hubris, grief, pity, guilt, and judgment before those forces govern blindly. The atlas already names the heart as a civic object of theater (`website/src/content/docs/lens.md:97`). The ritual seed should not absorb tragedy or catharsis. Its question is narrower: why does the public form let heart-knowledge become a shared civic event rather than only private recognition?

### Taboo

Taboo owns forbidden boundaries, public transgression, sacrifice, sovereignty transfer, and scapegoat purification. The Roman triumph already appears there as public killing hidden inside state ceremony (`website/src/content/docs/lens/taboo-as-control-surface.md:34`). That is a stronger home when the mechanism is breach, pollution, no-retreat cohesion, sacrificial violence, or guilt movement.

Public ritual can use Roman triumph only when the active question is different: how the parade makes conquest, captives, army, general, Jupiter, and city into one memory scene.

### Sacred Machines

Sacred Machines owns material forms that bridge invisible order into public life: temple, pyramid, cave, monument, ritual center, school, currency system, or national institution (`website/src/content/docs/lens/sacred-machines.md:8`, `website/src/content/docs/lens/sacred-machines.md:10`). It already includes gathering devices, shared perception, common memory, and ritual synchronization (`website/src/content/docs/lens/sacred-machines.md:24`, `website/src/content/docs/lens/sacred-machines.md:36`, `website/src/content/docs/lens/sacred-machines.md:42`).

Public ritual should not become another sacred-machine page. It belongs only when the repeated action-form matters more than the object or built infrastructure.

### Nation, Civilization, And Stories Control

Nation owns the sacred national body, unity of will, school, welfare, industry, population, war, and sacrifice. Civilization owns the arrangement of memory, gods, economy, violence, sex, hierarchy, language, and survival into a human type. Stories Control owns frames and scripts that coordinate action without explicit command (`website/src/content/docs/lens.md:107`).

Public ritual can touch all three, but should not replace them. National parades belong to Nation unless the public ceremony itself is the mechanism. Civilizational ritual belongs to Civilization unless the public scene is the unit being compared. Propaganda and story scripts belong to Stories Control unless staged participation, spectatorship, or repeated public performance is doing the collective-memory work.

## Grounding / Provenance Review

No blocking grounding findings.

The proposal's core evidence is concentrated and appropriate:

- `video:predictive-history-tymnsa83nhy@transcript:v1#seg-0035` and `video:predictive-history-tymnsa83nhy@transcript:v1#seg-0036` support the Roman triumph comparison: conquered wealth, captives, soldiers, general, Jupiter's temple, and sacrifice.
- `video:predictive-history-tymnsa83nhy@transcript:v1#seg-0036` supports Greek theater building collective consciousness through community participation, spectatorship, judgment, and public contest.
- `video:predictive-history-tymnsa83nhy@transcript:v1#seg-0037` supports the Greek contrast: enemy perspective, empathy, perspective change, and imaginative civilization rather than slave parade or human sacrifice.

The proposal uses neighboring public pages for boundary placement rather than as proof that a public ritual page already exists. That is the right evidentiary posture.

Residual risk: the phrase "collective consciousness" can overexpand. Future links or promotion should require a concrete staged public form plus at least one public-making verb: participating, watching, judging, sacrificing, marching, performing, retelling, repeating, or making memory durable.

## Promotion Boundary

Do not promote yet.

Promotion to an atlas relation could become justified if at least one future mutation needs a shared public-form bridge that cannot be handled cleanly by World-Making Media, Human Heart, Taboo, Sacred Machines, Nation, Civilization, or Stories Control. Promotion to a full concept page should require broader recurrence than the Viking/Roman/Greek comparison and should preserve the violence of the examples: the same public machine can train empathy, courage, domination, sacrifice, loyalty, or imperial self-belief.

Until then, route by active mechanism:

- Greek theater as symbolic world-making, democratic consciousness, or heart-knowledge goes to World-Making Media and Human Heart.
- Roman triumph as forbidden killing, transgression, or sacrificial state ceremony goes to Taboo.
- Temples, pyramids, caves, ritual centers, monuments, and public architecture go to Sacred Machines.
- National ceremonies and parades go to Nation when they form the national body.
- Broad social order and human-type formation go to Civilization.
- Frames and scripts that coordinate action without explicit public staging go to Stories Control.

Use **Public Ritual Memory Machines** only when the source compares or foregrounds the public staged form as the machine that makes collective memory, judgment, sacrifice, or imagination durable.
