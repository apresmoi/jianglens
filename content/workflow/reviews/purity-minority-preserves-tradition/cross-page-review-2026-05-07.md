## Decision

Hold **Purity Minority Preserves Tradition** as a proposal seed. Do not promote it to a public page or atlas relation yet.

The seed identifies a real mechanism in the Cyrus source: under exile and assimilation pressure, ordinary social adaptation is easier than continuity, while a minority can preserve a tradition by intensifying purity, law, ritual, textual unity, and sacrifice. That mechanism is strong enough to record because it would otherwise be split across legitimacy fiction, eschatology, administrative religion, and world-making media. It is not yet strong enough for a public surface because the current evidence cluster is primarily one lecture.

The useful current role is boundary discipline. Route future source moments to this seed only when the passage carries the full survival-through-separation mechanism:

- assimilation or adaptation is the easier path;
- a minority reads compromise as dissolution;
- purity, law, ritual, text, or separation hardens identity;
- continuity depends on costly refusal, exclusion, or willingness to sacrifice.
