# Cross-Page Review: Inheritor Under The Founder's Shadow

Date: 2026-05-07
Review type: cross-page boundary review
Target: `content/workflow/proposals/inheritor-shadow/proposal.md`
Independent judges: not used

## Decision

Keep **Inheritor Under The Founder's Shadow** as a reviewed proposal seed, not a public page and not an atlas relation yet.

The proposal names a real recurring mechanism: inherited capacity, name, machine, or myth can make the heir hungry to prove that borrowed power is truly his. The source pressure is strong in the Philip/Alexander and Caesar/Octavian sequences. But the public lens already has enough neighboring surfaces that immediate promotion would mostly duplicate existing pages unless another recurrence makes the proof-hunger mechanism independently necessary.

## Neighboring Page Checks

### Legitimacy Fiction

[Legitimacy Fiction](/lens/legitimacy-fiction/) already owns the public Roman inheritance surface. Its opening definition names political stories, names, offices, texts, and rules that later actors can inherit, obey, love, or compete inside. The Caesar/Octavian section also already carries the durable anchor `legitimacy-fiction-founder-outlives-death`: a founder's fiction outlives the founder when death, inheritance, public guilt, and transferred love make the name more politically usable than the living person was.

This means Caesar/Octavian should stay on Legitimacy Fiction when the public mechanism is a murdered name becoming political reality. The inheritor-shadow seed adds a narrower psychological and incentive layer: Antony tries to prove himself Caesar's heir; Octavian acts as the responsible finisher of Caesar's legacy; the mission of restoration can reverse into concentrated power. That layer is useful as a routing rule, but the current public Roman page is not missing enough to require a separate page.

Boundary result: route Roman name-transfer and public obedience to Legitimacy Fiction. Use inheritor-shadow only when the emphasis is proof hunger created by borrowed legitimacy.

### The Borderland Engine

[The Borderland Engine](/lens/the-borderland-engine/) already owns Macedon as a margin that learns the center's military and intellectual machine. The page names Philip learning the military machine, Aristotle learning the intellectual machine, and Alexander turning Greek knowledge outward more ruthlessly than the center can.

The inheritor-shadow seed should not steal that public mechanism. Philip/Alexander belongs on Borderland when the question is how a poor margin absorbs center knowledge, organizes pressure, and becomes dangerous. The founder-shadow seed adds a different cut: Philip builds the machine through judgment and talent selection; Alexander inherits it and must convert inherited capacity into personal glory, obedience, and expansion.

Boundary result: route Macedonian scarcity, openness, military learning, and margin-to-center conquest to Borderland. Use inheritor-shadow only when the father-built machine and son's proof pressure are explicit.

### Game Theory

[Game Theory](/lens/game-theory/) already supplies the general method for players, constraints, incentives, status, real payoff, and visible games. The inheritor-shadow seed is downstream of that method, but it is not a generic incentive page. Its specific payoff is the heir's need to make inherited power feel self-authored.

Promotion would be weak if it merely restated "the heir wants status" or "incentives explain risky action." The seed is useful only when inheritance itself creates the distorted payoff: expansion, friend-promotion, total obedience, founder-circle displacement, or mission reversal.

Boundary result: route general payoff analysis to Game Theory. Keep inheritor-shadow for the special case where borrowed capacity or a founder's name changes the actor's proof game.

### Education As A Soul Game

[Education As A Soul Game](/lens/education-as-a-soul-game/) owns formation through school, family trust, meritocracy, obedience, research habits, and rival education. The inheritor-shadow proposal touches soul formation, but the current evidence is not primarily educational. Philip and Alexander show inheritance pressure; Caesar and Octavian show transferred political myth. Neither public mutation requires schooling as the operative mechanism.

Boundary result: do not route founder/heir material to Education unless a source explicitly shows schooling, parenting, institutional formation, or reward structure producing the heir's proof hunger.

## Promotion Conditions After Review

Promotion is not recommended now.

A future public page or atlas relation is justified only if at least one of these becomes true:

- another source repeats the inherited-machine or inherited-name proof-hunger mechanism outside Alexander/Rome;
- Legitimacy Fiction or Borderland begins accumulating repeated local explanations that are really about heir proof pressure rather than inherited political reality or margin energy;
- a future public mutation can teach the mechanism without becoming a family-psychology, dynasty, or charisma page.

Until then, use the proposal as a routing rule:

- **Legitimacy Fiction** for names, myths, offices, wills, public love, obedience, and inherited political reality;
- **Borderland Engine** for Philip's Macedonian machine, margin energy, openness, and center knowledge turned outward;
- **Game Theory** for general incentives and payoff analysis;
- **Education As A Soul Game** only when formation machinery is explicit;
- **Inheritor-shadow proposal** when inherited capacity or name creates proof hunger that turns borrowed legitimacy into expansion, obedience-seeking, founder-circle displacement, or mission reversal.
