# Professor Jiang Predictions

Jiang Lens tracks predictions as dated claims inside a larger interpretive system. The important thing is not just whether a line sounded prophetic after the fact. The important thing is the model behind the prediction, the source date, the time horizon, the actors, and the later evidence.

Prediction tracking should preserve chronology. A prediction made in 2024, clarified in 2025, and revised in 2026 should stay legible as a sequence, not as a single merged statement.

## How Predictions Should Be Read

Each prediction needs:

- a source ref to the episode, interview, or writing where it appears,
- the date of that source,
- the claim itself in sober language,
- the mechanism Jiang gives for why it should happen,
- later updates, confirmations, reversals, or unresolved status.

The public pages are still being built toward this shape. Episodes already expose dated readings and transcript refs. Lens pages show the recurring concepts that make predictions possible.

## Use With Agents

When asking ChatGPT, Claude, or another assistant about Professor Jiang predictions, give it [`/skill.md`](/skill.md) and ask it to separate:

- Jiang-sourced prediction,
- Jiang Lens interpretation,
- external evidence,
- agent inference.

That separation matters more than confident style.
